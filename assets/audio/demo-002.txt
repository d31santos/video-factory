Here's the one AI mistake that quietly costs clinics hours every week. A team rolls out a shiny new tool, and then trusts its output blind. The fix is simple, but it's easy to skip. First, never ship AI output unchecked. Treat every draft as a first pass, not a final answer. Second, build a human review step into every workflow. One person confirms before anything reaches a patient or a chart. Third, measure the time you actually save, not just the tasks you automate. If a tool adds review work, it isn't really saving you anything. Do these three things, and AI becomes a reliable teammate instead of a risk.
